line of bugs · palette candidates

5 candidate palettes for the 16 insect orders + fallback, after deep research on Sanrio characters, Sailor Moon senshi, sweet-lolita, Y2K-girly, NewJeans-doll-cute, and pastel-goth references. Each candidate is shown with its 17-color swatch row, an autocomplete mock, and a gallery-tile mock on the eagle dark surface.

Distilled color theory of cuteness — 7 principles from the research

  • Pink is the anchor, not just a color. Authentic cute = 30-60% of visual weight in pink/rose/coral.
  • At least one "broken" off-spectrum dusty shade. Mauve, milk-tea, dusty plum. Signals "this was chosen, not defaulted."
  • Warm temperature dominance — the "strawberry milk" effect. Even when including blue, shift toward periwinkle/lavender.
  • Two anchors + accent constellation. Never 16 equal hues. 2 dominants + 14 supporting satellites.
  • Three sub-modes: shoujo / kawaii / doll-cute. Soft-dreamy / poppy-playful / dusty-vintage. Pick a lane.
  • Dark theme cute = mid-lightness tinted-mid, not full pastels. L≈65-75, not 85+. Pastels bleach on black; "pastel-goth" pinks are actually neon-leaning.
  • SaaS-pastel anti-pattern: equal saturation across all hues. That reads as "system color wheel" not "palette of objects."

Research recommendation

Candidate B (Pastel Goth Kawaii) — the only palette explicitly designed for cute-on-dark. Kuromi/Cinnamoroll merchandise is the proof-of-concept that you can have girly-cute and dark theme simultaneously. Insects are already a slightly macabre subject; a palette with a little edge gives cockroaches and earwigs dignity instead of a punishment color. Art students will recoil from condescending pastels; pastel-goth is the cute palette that's already passed teen taste-tests.

Fallback alternates if B isn't right: C for "I want quiet & sophisticated", A for "I want ambient sweetness", D for "I want maximum joy", E if Sailor Moon narrative speaks to you.
